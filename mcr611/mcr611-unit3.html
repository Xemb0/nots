Gram‚ÄëPositive Cocci

Staphylococcus (esp. S. aureus)

Virulence: Protein A, coagulase, hemolysins, leukocidins, toxins (TSST‚Äë1), biofilm (S. epidermidis). Diseases: Skin/soft tissue infections, abscess, osteomyelitis, endocarditis, pneumonia, food poisoning (preformed toxin). Dx: Gram+ cocci in clusters, catalase+, coagulase+ (aureus), mannitol salt agar yellow. Tx: Anti‚Äëstaphylococcal penicillins; MRSA: vancomycin/linezolid/daptomycin; source control.

Neisseria meningitidis, N. gonorrhoeae

Acid‚ÄëFast Bacteria, Spirochaetes, and Obligate Intracellular Pathogens

Mycobacterium tuberculosis

Pathogenesis: Intracellular survival in macrophages; granuloma formation. Dx: AFB smear/culture, NAAT, IGRA/TST, CXR. Tx: RIPE regimen (rifampicin, isoniazid, pyrazinamide, ethambutol) with DOT; manage drug resistance as per guidelines. Prevention: BCG vaccine, airborne precautions.

Spirochaetes

Treponema pallidum (Syphilis): Primary chancre ‚Üí secondary rash (palms/soles) ‚Üí latent ‚Üí tertiary (gummas, neuro, cardio). Dx: Nontreponemal (VDRL/RPR) + treponemal tests. Tx: Penicillin G.

Borrelia burgdorferi (Lyme): Erythema migrans, neuro/cardia/arthritis. Tx: Doxycycline; ceftriaxone for severe neuro/cardiac.

Leptospira: Biphasic illness; Weil‚Äôs disease (jaundice, renal failure). Tx: Doxycycline/penicillin.

Rickettsia

Obligate intracellular, arthropod‚Äëborne; vasculitis with fever and rash (e.g., Rocky Mountain spotted fever). Tx: Doxycycline (even in children for severe disease).

Chlamydia

Energy parasites; elementary/reticulate body cycle. C. trachomatis: NGU, cervicitis, PID, neonatal conjunctivitis/pneumonia, trachoma. Tx: Doxycycline/azithromycin (treat partners; consider co‚Äëtreatment for gonorrhea).

Quick Diagnosis & Management Pearls

Pattern Recognition
  • Clustered Gram+ cocci with pus ‚Üí think Staphylococcus aureus.
  • Sore throat + tender nodes + no cough ‚Üí Group A Strep pharyngitis.
  • Community pneumonia + rust sputum ‚Üí S. pneumoniae.
  • Watery diarrhea epidemic ‚Üí Vibrio cholerae/ETEC; bloody diarrhea ‚Üí Shigella/EHEC.
  • Meningitis with petechiae ‚Üí N. meningitidis.
