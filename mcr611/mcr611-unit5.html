Overview: Fungal and Protozoal Diseases

Scope: Dermatophytes, dimorphic fungi, opportunistic fungi (Candida, Pneumocystis) and endemic mycoses (Blastomycosis, Histoplasmosis); protozoa (Plasmodium, Trypanosoma, Entamoeba, Balantidium).

Dermatophytes (Tinea/Ringworm)

Agents: Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton. Infect keratinized tissues (skin, hair, nails). Clinical: Tinea corporis, capitis, pedis, cruris, unguium. Dx: KOH wet mount (septate hyphae), Wood lamp (some Microsporum). Tx: Topical azoles/terbinafine; nail/scalp often need oral terbinafine/itraconazole.

Opportunistic and Dimorphic Fungi

Candida albicans

Mucocutaneous (thrush, vaginitis) and invasive disease (candidemia). Risk: Antibiotics, devices, diabetes, neutropenia. Dx: Budding yeast with pseudohyphae; germ tube test (albicans). Tx: Fluconazole for mucosal; echinocandin for invasive; remove source.

Pneumocystis jirovecii (PCP)

HIV or immunosuppressed; subacute fever, dry cough, dyspnea; hypoxemia. Dx: Induced sputum/BAL with silver stain; ‚ÜëLDH; Œ≤‚ÄëD‚Äëglucan+. Tx: TMP‚ÄëSMX; add steroids if PaO2 low; prophylaxis when CD4<200.

Dimorphic Fungi: Histoplasma & Blastomyces

Histoplasma: Bird/bat droppings; intracellular yeasts in macrophages. Blastomyces: Broad‚Äëbased budding yeast; skin/bone lesions. Tx: Itraconazole (mild‚Äëmod), amphotericin B (severe).

Protozoal Infections

Plasmodium (Malaria)

Fever cycles (tertian/quartan), anemia, splenomegaly; falciparum severe. Dx: Thick/thin smear or rapid antigen. Tx: Chloroquine if sensitive; ACTs commonly; primaquine for vivax/ovale hypnozoites (G6PD test).

Trypanosoma

HAT (T. brucei): Tsetse fly; recurring fever, lymphadenopathy, CNS disease. Tx: Suramin (blood), melarsoprol (CNS). Chagas (T. cruzi): Reduviid bug; cardiomyopathy, megacolon. Tx: Benznidazole or nifurtimox (acute/early).

Entamoeba histolytica

Dysentery, liver abscess (‚Äúanchovy sauce‚Äù). Dx: Cysts/trophozoites in stool; serology for extraintestinal. Tx: Metronidazole + luminal agent.

Balantidium coli

Large ciliate protozoan; pig reservoir; colitis with dysentery. Tx: Tetracycline (preferred) or metronidazole.
