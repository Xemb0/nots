1. Isolation of Chromosomal and Plasmid DNA from Bacteria and Yeast

1.1 Isolation of Chromosomal DNA from Bacteria

Isolating bacterial chromosomal DNA is the first step in genetic engineering. The process involves breaking open bacterial cells and separating DNA from other cellular components.

Steps for Bacterial Chromosomal DNA Isolation:
  1. Cell Harvesting: Centrifuge bacterial culture to collect cells
  2. Cell Lysis: Break open cells using:
    • Physical methods: Freeze-thaw, sonication, or mechanical disruption
    • Chemical methods: Lysozyme (breaks cell wall) + detergents (SDS) to break membrane
  3. Protein Removal: Add proteinase K and incubate to digest proteins
  4. DNA Extraction: Use phenol-chloroform extraction to separate DNA from proteins
  5. DNA Precipitation: Add ethanol or isopropanol to precipitate DNA
  6. Purification: Wash with 70% ethanol and resuspend in buffer

5.2 Mechanism of DNA Ligation

T4 DNA Ligase Mechanism (3 Steps):
  1. Activation:
    • Ligase binds to ATP (or NAD+ for E. coli ligase)
    • Adenosine monophosphate (AMP) is transferred to lysine residue in ligase
    • Formation of ligase-AMP complex
  2. AMP Transfer:
    • AMP is transferred from ligase to 5' phosphate of DNA
    • Formation of DNA-AMP intermediate (pyrophosphate bond)
    • This activates the 5' phosphate group
  3. Phosphodiester Bond Formation:
    • The activated 5' phosphate attacks the 3' OH group
    • Phosphodiester bond is formed
    • AMP is released
    • Nick is sealed!

6.1 Linkers

Linkers are short, double-stranded DNA oligonucleotides that contain one or more restriction enzyme recognition sites.

How Linkers Work:
  1. Synthesize linkers with desired restriction site (e.g., EcoRI site)
  2. Phosphorylate linkers at 5' end using polynucleotide kinase
  3. Ligate linkers to blunt-ended DNA fragments (using T4 DNA ligase)
  4. Cut with restriction enzyme (e.g., EcoRI) to create sticky ends

6.3 Homopolymer Tails

Homopolymer tailing (also called dG-dC tailing) uses complementary homopolymeric sequences to join DNA fragments.

How Homopolymer Tailing Works:
  1. Add poly(dG) to vector:
    • Cut vector to create 3' OH ends
    • Add multiple G nucleotides using terminal deoxynucleotidyl transferase (TdT)
    • Creates vector with poly(dG) tails
  2. Add poly(dC) to insert:
    • Prepare insert DNA with 3' OH ends
    • Add multiple C nucleotides using TdT
    • Creates insert with poly(dC) tails
  3. Hybridization:
    • Mix vector and insert
    • Poly(dG) and poly(dC) anneal (G-C base pairing)
    • Forms hybrid molecule
  4. Gap Repair:
    • Gaps filled by DNA polymerase
    • Nicks sealed by DNA ligase
    • Recombinant DNA complete!
