1. Fermentor Design

A fermentor (or bioreactor) is a vessel designed to support the growth of microorganisms under controlled conditions. Different types of fermentors are used based on the specific requirements of the process.

Key Components:

  • Vessel (tank) - holds the culture medium
  • Impeller - mixes the medium and provides agitation
  • Sparger - introduces air/oxygen into the medium
  • Baffles - prevent vortex formation and improve mixing
  • Cooling jacket - maintains temperature
  • Probes - monitor pH, temperature, DOтВВ

Advantages: Versatile, good mixing, easy to scale up

2. Instrumentation and Control: Large Scale Production

Modern fermentors are equipped with various sensors and control systems to monitor and maintain optimal conditions for microbial growth and product formation.

3. Aeration and Agitation: Oxygen Transfer Kinetics

Oxygen transfer is critical for aerobic fermentations. Understanding oxygen transfer kinetics helps optimize aeration and agitation to meet the oxygen demand of microorganisms.

  • Liquid volume

Higher air flow rate increases oxygen availability but may cause foaming.

Viscosity, surface tension, and composition affect oxygen solubility and transfer.

4. Rheology of Fermentation Broths

Rheology is the study of flow and deformation of fluids. Understanding the rheological properties of fermentation broths is important for mixing, heat transfer, and oxygen transfer.
